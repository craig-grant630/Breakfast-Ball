St Andrews

St Andrews Old Course, located in St Andrews, Scotland, is one of the most iconic and historic golf courses in the world. Often referred to as the "Home of Golf," it has been a key venue in the history of the sport since the early 15th century. The Old Course is renowned for its challenging layout, including the famous Swilcan Bridge, the Hell Bunker, and the Road Hole. It has hosted The Open Championship numerous times, making it a revered destination for golfers and enthusiasts alike. The course's blend of tradition, scenic beauty, and competitive challenge makes it a bucket-list experience for players worldwide.

Augusta

Augusta National Golf Club in Georgia is one of the most iconic and revered golf courses globally, known for hosting the prestigious Masters Tournament each April. The course is celebrated for its stunning beauty, featuring lush fairways, vibrant azaleas, towering pine trees, and immaculate white sand bunkers, creating a serene and visually striking environment. Notable holes like those in "Amen Corner" have become legendary for their challenge and significance. Founded in 1933 by golf legend Bobby Jones and Clifford Roberts, Augusta National is steeped in tradition and history, making it a symbol of golfing excellence.

Carnoustie

Carnoustie Golf Links, located on the east coast of Scotland, is one of the most challenging and revered golf courses in the world, often earning the nickname "Carnasty" for its difficulty. Established in 1842, the course is famous for its narrow fairways, deep bunkers, and the often fierce coastal winds that test even the best golfers. The course has hosted The Open Championship multiple times, with its most famous moment being Jean Van de Velde's dramatic collapse on the 18th hole in 1999. Carnoustie's blend of history, difficulty, and rugged beauty make it a true test of golfing skill.
